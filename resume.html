PUBLISHED PAPERS

 

 

2022

The Effects of Overnight Events on Daytime Trading Sessions

Ham, H., Ryu, D., & Webb, R. I. International Review of Financial Analysis

 

 

 

Abstract: This study investigates the association between overnight and daytime-trading session returns in U.S. equity markets over the last 14 years and interpret it using the overreaction hypothesis. To identify the effects of overnight overreactions on daytime trading sessions, we control for daily investor sentiment, firms’ fundamental variables, and risk factors. Our results suggest that investors tend to overreact overnight and react more dispassionately during daytime trading sessions. Investors’ reactions also differ across sectors, and the degree of overreaction is greater in cyclical industries than in defensive industries. Additionally, we analyze the impacts of overnight reactions on daytime trading sessions focusing on recession periods. The impacts differ by subperiods and are pronounced during the Global Financial Crisis and the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic. Finally, we examine whether investors’ reactions to overnight news events respond differently to demand and supply shocks.

 

 

2019

Time‐Series Momentum in China’s Commodity Futures Market

Ham, H., Cho, H., Kim, H., & Ryu, D. Journal of Futures Market

 

 

 

Abstract: This study examines the time-series momentum in China's commodity futures market. We find that a time-series momentum strategy outperforms classical passive long and cross-sectional momentum strategies in terms of the Sharpe ratio, risk-adjusted excess returns, and cumulative returns. The time-series momentum strategy with a 1-month look-back period and a 1-month holding period exhibits the best performance. We observe clear time-series momentum patterns and find that the time-series momentum strategy is effective in the China's commodity futures market. However, the momentum lasts for less time in China than in the United States because China's futures market seems to have a greater number of speculative investors.

Are Analyst Recommendations Recommended?

Lee, J., Batten, J. A., Ham, H., & Ryu, D. (under review)

 

Summary: Analyzing the US equity (NYSE, AMEX, and NASDAQ) market dataset over the past 25 years, this study constructs the portfolios based on analyst recommendations of the stocks. We evaluate the performance of portfolios with analyst recommendation levels and changes, and then we compare them by periods, market capitalizations, and sectors. Additionally, we compare the performance of the analyst recommendation change portfolio with the momentum strategy, which is a representative strategy using a technical indicator that has a low cost to access information. We find that the momentum portfolio outperforms the recommendation change portfolio across periods, market capitalizations, and sectors. (will be updated)

 

 

Rolling Attention for Intraday Stock Price Prediction

Lee, W., Ham, H., & Lee, J. (in the final stage of completion)

 

 

Summary: Attention-based models have been gaining popularity recently for their strong performance demonstrated in various fields. As of 2021, the Temporal Fusion Transformer (Lim et al., 2019) is marked as the SOTA (State Of The Art) model in time series forecasting. In this paper, we predict intraday prices of the stocks in Dow 30 with premarket trading data. We modified the Temporal Fusion Transformer to fit financial analysis by changing the Variable Selection Networks structure, and we consider the Known Future Inputs that are designed to reflect the characteristics of financial markets. A Design of Multi-Tasking Learning for Trading Execution

Ham, H., Lee, W., Hwang, S., & Kim, H. (in progress of data processing)

 

 

Summary: One of the most important factors to consider when executing a large number of orders is the price impact on the market. In particular, block trading would cause huge losses when the market has low liquidity because of the price impact. In this paper, we suggest a model to reduce the price impact in the execution of large orders. We design the model combining two different purpose models to overcome the difficulties in simultaneously learning the overall flow and short-term movement of stock prices. We consider the Transformer-based model to learn the overall flow of stock prices and the Implicit Quantile Networks model (Dbney at el., 2018) for the short-term movements. In addition, we measure the price impact adopted from the study of Cont and Stoikov (Journal of Financial Economics, 2010), which reflects market imbalance, and we adjust that model to the reward function in the reinforcement learning model.
